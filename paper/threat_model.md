# Threat Model

Odyssey models defenders operating cloud or enterprise telemetry pipelines during mixed cryptographic transition periods. Threats include conventional attacks, stealthy low-and-slow activity, noisy degradations that increase epistemic uncertainty, and metadata patterns suggestive of future cryptographic exposure such as crypto-suite mismatch or archival sensitivity.

The framework assumes passive observation of telemetry or event-window aggregates. It does not assume packet payload decryption and does not claim protection against cryptographic breakage itself. Instead, it studies whether transition fragility and uncertainty signals improve detection prioritization.
